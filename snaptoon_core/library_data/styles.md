## Fumetto (20)

### Brutalist Graphic Novel
Visual style: brutalist graphic novel with heavy aggressive linework. Style references: stark black and white contrasts, geometric panel compositions, raw urban gritty feel, thick aggressive ink strokes, distorted perspectives, claustrophobic compositions. Technique: heavy black ink, no color or extremely limited flat tones, maximum shadow fill. Mood: confrontational, political, oppressive. Influenced by Frank Miller and David Mazzucchelli raw period work. Constraints: no pretty illustration no decorative elements pure graphic weight.

### Cinematic Storyboard Style
Visual style: professional cinematic storyboard. Style references: rough sketchy linework indicating motion and camera angles, gray-scale wash shading, wide cinematic panels, dynamic action blocking, clear staging. Technique: pencil or marker sketch quality, directional gray washes, clean character blocking over detailed rendering. Look: film pre-production professional quality, implied camera movement through composition. Constraints: no finished illustration quality intentionally rough and functional.

### Franco-Belga Classico
Visual style: classic Franco-Belgian bande dessinée. Style references: Hergé ligne claire influence with more expressive character work, clean precise linework, balanced compositions, bright flat watercolor fills, clear readable European panel design. Technique: precise technical linework with uniform weight, clean color fills within strong outlines, architectural background accuracy. Palette: bright balanced classic comics colors. Influenced by Tintin, Spirou — European clarity and draftsmanship. Constraints: no manga influence no American superhero anatomy.

### Golden Age Comics
Visual style: American Golden Age comic books (1938–1956). Style references: bold primary color printing with visible halftone dot patterns, thick black outlines, dynamic action poses, dramatic foreshortening, heroic anatomy, newsprint texture. Technique: four-color printing limitation as aesthetic, strong silhouettes, patriotic palette (red, blue, gold), Ben-Day dot patterns. Mood: heroic, optimistic, melodramatic. Constraints: period-accurate visual conventions no modern digital rendering.

### Gothic Horror Comics
Visual style: dark gothic comics with atmospheric chiaroscuro. Style references: heavy shadow work, dramatic contrast, aged Victorian settings, intricate organic detail, scratchy crosshatching. Technique: ink crosshatching and brush work, muted palette (greens, purples, bone white, rust), dramatic atmospheric compositions. Mood: tension, melancholy, the uncanny. Constraints: no bright cheerful colors no simple clean lines deep dramatic atmosphere only.

### Graphic Novel Europea
Visual style: modern European literary graphic novel. Style references: sophisticated loose linework, complex color palettes, psychologically rich compositions, mature literary sensibility. Influenced by Moebius, Enki Bilal, Joann Sfar. Technique: confident expressive line, complex atmospheric color, page-as-composition thinking. Mood: literary, adult, culturally specific. Constraints: no genre conventions no superhero influence.

### Heavy Ink Noir
Visual style: extreme heavy ink noir. Style references: Frank Miller Sin City aesthetic — pure black and white, maximum shadow fill, near-silhouette compositions, dramatic spotlight effects, rain-slicked streets. Technique: brush and ink, no halftones, pure black areas dominant with precise white highlights only. Mood: brutal, cinematic, high contrast moral universe. Constraints: black and white ONLY no grey tones use pure black and white only maximum graphic impact.

### Ink Wash Illustration
Visual style: traditional East Asian ink wash comic. Style references: sumi-e brush tradition applied to narrative sequential art, flowing brush strokes, spontaneous ink washes, negative space as compositional element. Technique: black ink with grey tonal washes, subtle paper texture, calligraphic quality in linework, no mechanical precision. Mood: meditative, expressive, culturally specific. Constraints: no western comics conventions embrace brush spontaneity and negative space.

### Ligne Claire
Visual style: pure Hergé ligne claire. Style references: uniform weight clean outlines with no variation in line weight, bright local colors within perfectly clear boundaries, no shadows no crosshatching, even flat lighting. Technique: mechanical precision in line, Belgian clear-line tradition, clean backgrounds with architectural accuracy. Palette: bright primary and secondary flat fills. Mood: rational, clear, optimistic. Constraints: absolutely no shadow work no varied line weight perfect clarity only.

### Manga Seinen
Visual style: seinen manga for adult Japanese comics. Style references: Vagabond, Berserk, 20th Century Boys — detailed realistic anatomy, complex screen tone shading, dynamic speed lines, expressive black and white contrast. Technique: sophisticated panel composition, cinematic angles, detailed environments, psychologically complex character expressions, varied line weight. Mood: adult, complex, literary. Constraints: no shōnen simplification adult narrative weight and draftsmanship.

### Minimal Line Art Comics
Visual style: minimal single-weight line art comics. Style references: Jeffrey Brown, Chris Ware minimalist register — maximum expressiveness with fewest possible lines, intimate personal conversational quality. Technique: single weight clean outline, minimal detail, simple or absent backgrounds, white space as storytelling element. Mood: intimate, honest, unguarded. Constraints: resist adding detail embrace imperfection and simplicity.

### Neo-Noir Cinematic
Visual style: contemporary neo-noir comic with cinematic color. Style references: desaturated scene with selective vivid accent color (typically red or yellow in near-monochrome environment), digital painting quality, film-still compositions, wide panels. Technique: sophisticated color grading applied to comics format, neon reflections on wet pavement, high contrast with selective warmth. Mood: modern noir, morally complex, aesthetically precise. Constraints: no full color saturation use selective color purposefully.

### Noir Graphic Novel
Visual style: classic noir graphic novel. Style references: David Lapham Stray Bullets, classic crime comics — deep shadow fill, rain and neon, fedora silhouettes, femme fatale compositions. Technique: black dominant with spot reds and yellows as accent, gritty urban texture, architectural detail serving atmosphere. Mood: gritty, morally ambiguous, rain-soaked urban poetry. Constraints: limited palette black dominant with maximum two accent colors.

### Pulp Comics
Visual style: Golden and Silver Age pulp superhero comics. Style references: bright bold colors, melodramatic compositions, speed lines and action effects, classic superhero anatomy. Technique: dynamic impossible action poses, four-color printing palette, vintage newsprint feel with visible printing grain and registration shifts. Mood: heroic, melodramatic, kinetic. Constraints: embrace period conventions and visual excess over modern restraint.

### Retro Sci-Fi Pulp
Visual style: 1950s–1960s science fiction pulp magazine illustration. Style references: Amazing Stories, Galaxy Science Fiction covers — rocketships, chrome robots, atomic age optimism, painted illustration style. Technique: gouache or oil paint illustration aesthetic, bold primary colors with warm dramatic lighting. Mood: optimistic Cold War era space race wonder. Constraints: period-accurate retro-futurism no modern sci-fi aesthetics.

### Rotoscope Animation Comics
Visual style: rotoscoped-from-live-action comic style. Style references: Ralph Bakshi, A-ha Take On Me — traced-over photographic reference creating hyper-realistic figure proportions with drawn quality. Technique: specific physical detail from life reference, uncanny valley proportions, sketchy rotoscoped line quality, photo-realistic backgrounds with drawn characters. Mood: uncanny, kinetic, between documentary and fantasy. Constraints: embrace the slight wrongness of rotoscope do not correct toward idealized anatomy.

### Shōnen Anime
Visual style: classic shōnen manga and anime. Style references: Dragon Ball, Naruto, One Piece — large expressive eyes, dynamic action poses, speed lines and impact effects, sweat drops and emotion symbols. Technique: clean digital or ink line with flat fills and cel shading, dramatic power-up compositions. Mood: energetic, optimistic, power-escalating. Constraints: young energy no seinen complexity or darkness.

### Underground Comix
Visual style: 1960s–70s underground alternative comix. Style references: classic alternative comix tradition — intentionally raw and non-conformist linework, surreal psychedelic imagery, counter-culture visual language. Technique: obsessive crosshatching detail, exaggerated caricature style, newsprint zine aesthetic. Mood: psychedelic, politically charged, anti-establishment. Constraints: embrace deliberate raw quality reject mainstream comics polish.

### Watercolor Comics
Visual style: watercolor-colored traditional comics. Style references: European album comics tradition — precise linework with loose wet watercolor fills bleeding slightly beyond lines. Technique: soft color washes, visible watermarks and blooms, paper texture, warm expressive color. Mood: literary, warm, hand-crafted. Constraints: preserve watercolor accidents and imperfections no digital flat fill perfection.

### Webtoon Coreano
Visual style: contemporary Korean webtoon digital style. Style references: popular manhwa platforms (Lezhin, Webtoon) — clean digital linework, full color with soft cell shading, vertical scroll format compositions, expressive character design with large eyes, contemporary fashion. Technique: clean precise digital inking, soft gradient shading, contemporary K-drama visual sensibility. Mood: emotional, contemporary, Instagram-aesthetic. Constraints: vertical composition thinking no page-format panel grids.

## Fotografia (20)

### Analog Film Photography
Authentic analog 35mm film photography. Kodak Portra, Fuji Pro 400H film stocks — film grain texture, warm color bias, subtle lens vignetting, accurate depth of field blur. No digital correction artifacts, natural imperfect exposure. Mood: warm, timeless, authentic. Constraints: must look like actual film scan no digital over-processing.

### Brutalist Photography
Brutalist architectural photography. Documentation of Corbusier, Rudolph, Lasdun buildings — raw concrete surfaces, stark geometric forms, harsh direct lighting revealing material texture. Monumental scale, cold institutional spaces, symmetrical oppressive compositions, midday harsh light. Mood: cold, powerful. Constraints: no softening of brutalist harshness embrace the severity.

### Chiaroscuro Photography
Dramatic chiaroscuro lighting photography. Rembrandt and Caravaggio painting aesthetic applied to photography — strong single-source directional light creating deep shadows. High contrast, rich shadow detail, subject emerging from darkness. Mood: dramatic, psychological, timeless. Constraints: single dominant light source only dramatic contrast non-negotiable.

### Cinematic Realism
Contemporary cinematic photography. Roger Deakins, Emmanuel Lubezki cinematography — anamorphic lens characteristics, specific film LUT color grading, golden hour or magic hour lighting. Movie still quality, narrative moment captured, environmental storytelling. Mood: narrative, atmospheric, prestige cinema. Constraints: must look like a frame from a serious film not a commercial or fashion shoot.

### Cyberpunk Neon
Cyberpunk city night photography. Blade Runner, Ghost in the Shell live-action — dense neon signage reflections in rain puddles, foggy atmosphere, high-rise dystopian architecture. Palette: electric blue, magenta, acid green neon on black. Mood: dystopian wonder. Constraints: perpetual night perpetual rain neon only.

### Dark Academia Photography
Dark academia aesthetic photography. Oxford, Cambridge, gothic university spaces — wood-paneled libraries, candle light on ancient texts, ivy-covered architecture. Palette: amber, sepia, deep wood brown, cream. Mood: melancholy intellectual beauty, autumnal longing. Constraints: genuine gothic academic spaces no modern clean environments.

### Documentary Realism
Cinéma vérité documentary photography. Cartier-Bresson, Sebastião Salgado, Mary Ellen Mark — available light only, natural imperfect exposure, real environments without staging. Decisive moment composition, truth over beauty, human dignity as primary subject. Constraints: absolutely no studio lighting no staging no model perfection real people real places.

### Dreamcore Photography
Dreamcore and liminal space aesthetic photography. r/LiminalSpace, backrooms aesthetic — slightly uncanny suburban or institutional environments, unusual lighting. Desaturated palette with single warm accent, empty corridors, pools, shopping malls between hours. Mood: nostalgia curdled into unease, the uncanny. Constraints: must feel slightly wrong embrace the liminal emptiness.

### Ethereal Fantasy Photography
High-key fantasy portrait photography. Anne Geddes ethereal tradition, pre-Raphaelite painting achieved photographically — soft diffused light, flowing fabrics, flower crowns, dreamy soft focus background. Palette: white, pale gold, blush pink, sage. Mood: idealized beauty, dreamy. Constraints: soft light only no harsh shadows no urban settings.

### Fine Art Portraiture
Museum-quality fine art portrait photography. Annie Leibovitz, Gregory Crewdson — controlled studio or location lighting, psychological depth in subject expression, deliberate costume and prop choices. Large format precision quality, timeless formal quality. Mood: psychological depth, narrative weight, timeless. Constraints: every element intentional nothing accidental no snapshot quality.

### Gothic Noir Photography
Gothic noir fashion photography. Tim Walker dark work, Nick Knight — Victorian architecture or aged industrial settings, blue-black color palette, theatrical mist. Palette: midnight blue, black, bone white, deep crimson accent. Mood: beautiful darkness, narrative mystery, romantic melancholy. Constraints: high production quality gothic locations atmospheric elegance only.

### Hard Flash Street Photography
Hard on-camera flash street photography. Bruce Gilden, Weegee, Martin Parr — on-camera flash in daylight creating overexposed foreground subjects against naturally exposed backgrounds. Hard shadow behind subject, saturated colors, confrontational proximity. Mood: documentary aggression, confrontational. Constraints: direct flash only no softboxes no flattery harsh and honest.

### Italian Giallo Aesthetic
Italian giallo film photography aesthetic. Dario Argento, Mario Bava — highly saturated candy colors (deep red, emerald green, cobalt blue), gloved hands, abstract detail compositions. Color gels on lighting, theatrical deep-red accents, baroque interior design. Palette: supersaturated primary and secondary colors especially deep red and emerald. Mood: beautiful tension, stylized. Constraints: maximum color saturation no naturalism no documentary quality.

### Minimal Geometric Photography
Minimalist geometric composition photography. Hiroshi Sugimoto, Rinko Kawauchi — extreme simplicity, one or two strong geometric shapes, perfect negative space. Precise composition with maximum reduction, muted or monochrome palette, Zen graphic clarity. Mood: silent, contemplative, Zen. Constraints: absolute removal of all non-essential elements.

### Neo-Noir Cinematic
Contemporary neo-noir cinematographic photography. Roger Deakins Blade Runner 2049, David Fincher — sophisticated color grading (teal shadows, orange skin tones or near-monochrome), rain-slicked urban environments. Palette: teal and orange or desaturated with amber accent. Mood: morally ambiguous, aesthetically precise. Constraints: film-quality production not fashion photography territory.

### Painterly Photography
Photography processed toward painting quality. Brooke Shaden, fine art composite photography — texture overlays suggesting oil or watercolor, controlled depth of field creating painted look. Mood: dreamy, artistic, between photography and painting. Constraints: must retain photographic origin while approaching painting aesthetic.

### Rainy Urban Cinematic
Urban rain with cinematic poetry. Wong Kar-wai rainy Hong Kong, Blade Runner Seattle — reflections in wet pavement doubling city lights, steam from grates, umbrella silhouettes against backlit fog. Palette: warm amber and neon on deep blue-black wet surfaces. Mood: melancholy urban poetry, romantic isolation. Constraints: rain is mandatory wet surfaces doubling the world.

### Retro-Futurism Photography
Retro-futuristic architecture and design photography. Eero Saarinen TWA terminal, Oscar Niemeyer Brasília, Googie architecture — chrome and plastic surfaces, optimistic 1960s-70s color palette. Palette: warm white, chrome, avocado, orange, sky blue. Mood: optimistic nostalgia for a future that never came. Constraints: authentic period locations or objects only.

### Surreal Editorial Photography
High-end editorial surrealist photography. Tim Walker, Eugenio Recuero — technically perfect execution of conceptually impossible scenarios. Luxury brand aesthetic applied to surrealist vision, impossible scale relationships rendered with photographic conviction. Mood: intelligent, otherworldly, expensive. Constraints: technical perfection mandatory the impossible must look utterly real.

### Vintage Kodak Portra
Vintage Kodak Portra 400 and 800 film aesthetic. 1990s–2000s professional film portrait photography — warm color bias (warm flesh tones, golden whites), beautiful grain structure, beautiful highlight rolloff. Film scan aesthetic, natural color rendering, generous exposure. Palette: warm cream whites, golden skin tones, gentle blues. Mood: warm, timeless, the best version of the everyday. Constraints: warm film authentic quality no cool or digital toning.

## Cinema (20)

### Analog Horror
Found footage and analog VHS aesthetic. Retro institutional video documentation — VHS degradation artifacts (tracking errors, color bleeding, static, date-time stamps). Low resolution institutional video camera quality, mundane familiar settings with eerie atmosphere. Palette: washed-out VHS colors, greens and ambers of old tape. Mood: tension through bureaucratic normalcy. Constraints: must look like real degraded analog video tape artifacts mandatory.

### Anamorphic Cinema
High-end anamorphic lens cinematography. Roger Deakins, Vilmos Zsigmond — distinctive horizontal oval bokeh, elegant anamorphic lens flares, wide panoramic aspect ratio (2.39:1). Shallow depth of field with anamorphic character, horizontal stretched bokeh. Mood: the look of serious expensive cinema. Constraints: anamorphic characteristics must be visible horizontal bokeh horizontal flares.

### Brutalist Sci-Fi
Brutalist architecture in science fiction context. Early Ridley Scott, Jodorowsky's Dune concept art — raw concrete and exposed steel at futuristic scale, Soviet constructivist aesthetic. Palette: concrete grey, rust, harsh white artificial light. Mood: dystopian grandeur, architecture as power. Constraints: brutalist architectural logic must drive all design decisions.

### Cyberpunk Cinematography
Cyberpunk cinematographic style. Blade Runner 2049 (Deakins), Ghost in the Shell 2017 — perpetual rainy night, dense neon signage, technological augmentation. Palette: teal shadows, electric magenta, acid yellow neon, deep black. Mood: technological sublime, dystopian beauty. Constraints: rain and neon mandatory no daytime scenes.

### Documentary Realism
Cinéma vérité documentary cinematography. Frederick Wiseman, Maysles Brothers, Joshua Oppenheimer — handheld camera naturalism, available light only, observational long takes. Non-staged reality composition, camera that disappears into reality. Constraints: no artificial lighting no staging handheld observational camera only.

### Dreamlike Auteur Cinema
European art cinema dreamlike quality. Tarkovsky Stalker/Solaris, Bergman Persona — slow deliberately paced images, symbolic imagery over narrative clarity. Light as metaphysical phenomenon, time dilated through long takes, landscape as psychological state. Mood: contemplative, spiritual, the weight of existence. Constraints: narrative clarity is not the goal symbolic resonance over explanation.

### Epic Historical Cinema
Epic historical cinema. Kubrick Barry Lyndon, Ridley Scott Gladiator — vast period-accurate environments, natural-source lighting with practical torches and candles. Historical painting aesthetic achieved cinematographically. Palette: natural historical — candlelight amber, daylight grey, sky blues. Mood: the weight of history. Constraints: natural light sources only period accuracy essential.

### French New Wave
French Nouvelle Vague cinema. Godard Breathless, Truffaut 400 Blows — jump cuts, location shooting making Paris a character, self-conscious cinema, actors looking at camera. Handheld spontaneity, existentialist intellectual atmosphere, black and white or desaturated. Mood: intellectual, youthful, politically engaged, anti-Hollywood. Constraints: location not studio handheld not locked embrace jump cut and anti-convention.

### German Expressionism
Weimar-era German Expressionist cinema. The Cabinet of Dr. Caligari, Nosferatu, The Golem — extreme diagonal architecture, painted shadow patterns, psychological projection into physical environment. Sets designed to express psychological states not realistic spaces, angular unreality. Palette: stark black and white with painted shadow. Constraints: expressionist logic must distort reality toward psychological truth.

### Gothic Horror
Gothic atmospheric cinema. Classic British Gothic films, James Whale Frankenstein — fog-filled moorland, candlelit castle interiors, cobweb-draped architecture, theatrical atmosphere. Palette: deep shadow, amber candlelight, moonlight blue, crimson accent. Mood: beautiful decaying grandeur, romantic melancholy. Constraints: theatrical Gothic production design embrace melodrama and fog.

### Grainy 70s Cinema
1970s American New Hollywood cinema. Vilmos Zsigmond McCabe and Mrs. Miller, Conrad Hall Chinatown — high ASA film stock visible grain, warm amber or desaturated brown-green palette. Naturalistic lighting without Hollywood glamour, location shooting in unglamorous American spaces. Palette: warm desaturated brown-amber or cold grey-green. Mood: paranoid, humanistic, post-idealistic. Constraints: heavy grain mandatory no clean digital look.

### Italian Neorealism
Italian Neorealist cinema. De Sica Bicycle Thieves, Rossellini Rome Open City — non-professional actors in real post-war Italian locations, available light only. Working-class or devastated urban environments, the face of real people as cinema's primary subject. Mood: humanistic witness, the dignity of ordinary suffering. Constraints: real locations real people available light only no studio.

### Neo Noir
Contemporary neo-noir cinema. Michael Mann Heat, David Fincher Seven — sophisticated color grading, contemporary urban locations, psychological complexity. Palette: controlled sophisticated grading — often teal-orange or near-monochrome with warm accent. Mood: morally complex, urban, psychological. Constraints: sophisticated contemporary execution no pastiche of classic noir.

### Noir Cinematic
Classic film noir cinematography. John Alton, Greg Toland — high contrast black and white, venetian blind shadow patterns, femme fatale lighting, rain-wet night streets reflecting neon. 1940s–50s Hollywood crime cinema visual language, cigarette smoke volumetric light.
